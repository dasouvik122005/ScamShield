About ScamShield

An open-source, AI-powered toolkit designed to protect job seekers from fraudulent recruitment postings.

Our Mission

Job scams are a growing global problem. According to the FBI's Internet Crime Report, victims lost over $367 million to employment scams in 2023 alone. These scams prey on vulnerable job seekers — from fresh graduates to experienced professionals.

ScamShield was built to democratize fraud detection. By combining Natural Language Processing, machine learning classifiers, and heuristic analysis, we give every job seeker the ability to verify a posting before applying — instantly and for free.

How It Works

1. Data Ingestion

You submit a job posting via URL scan (we auto-extract details) or manual input form. The raw text is assembled from all fields.

2. NLP Text Classification

The posting text is preprocessed (tokenization, stemming, lemmatization) and run through a TF-IDF vectorizer + Logistic Regression classifier trained on 18,000+ real job postings.

3. Feature Engineering

We compute 14+ engineered features: word count, readability scores, URL density, email domains, punctuation patterns, and location-based risk ratios.

4. Random Forest Classifier

The numeric/engineered features are fed into a Random Forest classifier that captures non-linear patterns like "high exclamation count + low word count + free email" combinations.

5. Ensemble Decision

Both model predictions are combined (60% ML weight, 40% heuristic rules) into a final trust score. Heuristic rules check for SSL, domain age, urgency language, and recruiter signals.

6. Risk Report

A comprehensive dashboard displays the trust score, pillar-by-pillar audit results, highlighted risk factors, and actionable recommendations.

Training Dataset

ScamShield was trained on the Employment Scam Aegean Dataset (EMSCAD) — a benchmark dataset containing 17,880 real-world job postings (866 fraudulent, 17,014 legitimate) collected from multiple job aggregation platforms.

The dataset includes structured fields such as job title, location, company profile, description, requirements, benefits, education level, experience level, telecommuting status, and more.

The dataset is publicly available on Kaggle.
